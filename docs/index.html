Problem statement

London travel gets harder when signal drops and directions get confusing.

Visitors often switch between map apps, translation tools, and station signs while trying to understand London's transport network. Underground, that experience breaks down fast.

The latest MVP direction in this repository stays focused on practical travel help: offline-first navigation, multilingual queries, local place resolution, and clear next steps when the route or surroundings feel unfamiliar.

Solution overview

One lightweight guide for navigating London from start to finish.

NAV AiDE combines offline maps, local routing assets, voice input and output, and on-device AI intent handling designed for London visitors. It is built to answer practical travel questions without depending on a constant mobile connection.

The app direction now centers on two core flows: GO for route search, transport selection, map display, and spoken guidance; and LOST? for recovering when the user is unsure where they are or what sign to follow next.

Feature highlights

Designed around the core travel tasks visitors need most.

140+ languages

Multilingual travel queries help visitors ask for directions naturally while keeping place names grounded in English.

Offline maps

Offline maps and local assets are designed to stay useful in tunnels, stations, and other low-signal environments.

Door-to-door directions

Door-to-door guidance is planned across walking and public transport for practical London journeys.

LOST? helper

A focused recovery flow helps identify nearby signs, stations, and clues to get the journey back on track quickly.

MVP scope

Built around clear travel flows instead of a broad general-purpose assistant.

GO flow

Route search, transport selection, map display, route cards, and text-to-speech playback.

LOST? flow

Resolve location clues locally and guide the user when they are unsure where they are.

Local-first AI

On-device speech and AI support for intent handling and response rendering without cloud AI APIs.

Architecture principles

Designed to keep core guidance available even in airplane mode.

  • Offline-first core routing, map rendering, POI search, voice I/O, and local AI inference.
  • Local resolution of stations, points of interest, and aliases before routing or response rendering.
  • No cloud AI for the MVP's core navigation, search, voice, or LLM inference experience.
