Problem statement

Tourists hit dead zones exactly when London becomes hard to read.

Traditional map apps assume a stable connection and a user who can already decode station signage, route changes, and local place names. That breaks down underground, in crowded interchanges, and when a visitor needs help in their own language.

Solution overview

A London guide that keeps its travel brain on the device.

NAV AiDE combines offline assets, local entity resolution, on-device response generation, and transport-specific routing so the MVP can keep working when the network does not. It is focused on guidance, recovery, and clarity instead of generic search results.
